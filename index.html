Case study 2

No specific signs


There are no specific signs in many of the open spaces. Without a clear identification, individuals are hard to notice the spaces, let alone enjoying them. One of the examples is Laguna Verde in Whampoa 8.

In a document provided by Lands Department, it is said that an open space with not less than 13,000 square meters is located at ground level. However, when our reporter got to the place, only a garden, which appears to be a residential one, is found.

The uncertainty stops people from stepping in, as they may have to bear legal responsibilities if they rush into private areas.
